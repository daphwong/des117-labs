Tablo

Biography

Tablo, not to be confused with the DVR Tablo, is also known as Daniel Armand Lee. He was born in July 22, 1980. Tablo is a Korean-Canadian rapper, record producer, songwriter, author, and entrepreneur who is currently based in South Korea. Tablo is best known as the leader and producer of the Korean hip-hop group Epik High, and the founder and CEO of independent music label HIGHGRND (High Ground).

Tablo became employed as a lyricist while still in high school. After finishing his studies, Tablo returned to South Korea to pursue music against his parents' wishes. They wanted him to become a lawyer but his love for music was far too great. In 2001, Tablo formed Epik High alongside DJ Tukutz and Mithra Jin. The group has since released 10 full-length albums and 1 EP, with Tablo having produced and co-written all the songs. He eventually had his own solo album, Fever's End, which was released in 2011.

Outside of Epik High, Tablo is a record producer for other artists and involved in collaborative projects in Korea such as Borderline, Eternal Morning, and Anyband. His music incorporates numerous styles, ranging from trance, trip hop, to rock. Tablo also published a book that became one of the best-selling books, called Pieces of You. It published in both English and Korean. He had some part in acting and debuted in films such as Nonstop (2005) and Fantastic Parasuicides (2007).
